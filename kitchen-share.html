Origin  ·  Ongoing
The Test Kitchen
...two questions I kept cooking toward, long before I called it a kitchen share.

It started with ferments and seitan, for different reasons. Ferments because they're a study in transformation and death — what a body owes the microbes it's made of, worked through alongside Lisa Heldke's writing on food and philosophy. Seitan because plant-based protein is a real, unglamorous problem worth solving well, and because "meat, but not meat" is its own strange question about taste and ethics.

Cooking that seriously produces a lot of food — more than one person can eat. Around the same time, a friend, Ava, needed good, novel, reasonably priced food, and I needed income to keep doing this work at all. That exchange is where the kitchen share actually started. It's since grown to six people, and it's still growing.

— Ferments — transformation, death, and what we owe the microbes we're made of
— Seitan — protein, ethics, and what "meat" even means
— The exchange — too much food, met by people who wanted it
— Now — six people, cooking and tasting together, and counting

None of this started as a pitch. It still isn't one.

Gathering  ·  Ongoing
What Happens Here
...cooking together, and tasting things before they have names.

We cook, eat what worked and what didn't, and talk about why. New batches get facilitated tastings before they're anything you could buy — that's not a metaphor, it's literally how the seitan and the fermented chili got their current shape. Nobody's performing for anybody. It's a kitchen, full for an evening, and then it isn't.

— Tastings — new batches get opinions before they get a name
— Co-design — members shape what actually gets made
— No pitch — nobody's selling anybody anything at the table
WhereBaltimore, MD
WhoWhoever's cooking that week
FormatInformal, recurring

"A kitchen share isn't a focus group. It's just people who show up, cook, and tell you honestly if something's good."
